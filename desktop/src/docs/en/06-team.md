# Chapter 6: Team and Collaboration 👥

JinnLog is not just for solo work. You can build a team, invite collaborators, and work together on your projects.

---

## 👥 The Team Page

Go to the **"Team"** page in the sidebar. Here you manage who has access to your projects.

### The Team Table
The main view is a table showing all members across your projects:

| Column | What it shows |
|--------|---------------|
| **Member** | Avatar (initials circle), display name, and username |
| **Account Type** | "JinnLog" badge (real user) or "Local" badge (virtual user) |
| **Project** | Which project this membership belongs to |
| **Role** | OWNER (golden badge) or EDITOR (blue badge) |
| **Actions** | Remove button (trash icon) — not available for project owners |

### Filtering by Project
Use the **project selector dropdown** at the top to show only members of a specific project, or choose "All projects" to see everyone.

---

## ➕ Adding Team Members

Click the **"+ New Member"** button in the top right corner. A dialog opens with:

### 1. Select a Project
First, choose which project you want to add the member to (required).

### 2. Choose the Member Type
A toggle switch lets you choose between two types:

#### 🔗 Connect JinnLog User (Friend)
Select this when you want to add a **real JinnLog user** from your contacts:
*   A dropdown shows all your connected JinnLoggers (friends).
*   Select one and click **"Add to Team"**.
*   They will receive access to the project.

> **⚠️ Note:** You must first connect with the user on the **JinnLoggers** page before you can add them to a team.

#### 👤 Create Local User (Virtual)
Select this when you need a **placeholder member** — someone who doesn't use JinnLog but needs to appear in your project:
*   Enter a **Display Name** (e.g., "John Doe (External)").
*   Enter a **Username Identifier** (e.g., "john.doe.ext") — must be unique.
*   Click **"Create Local"**.

Local users are useful for:
*   External stakeholders who don't use the app.
*   Assigning tasks to people not yet on JinnLog.
*   Planning and resource allocation before the team is fully onboarded.

### Understanding the Types
The **right panel** explains the difference:
*   **JinnLog User** (blue badge, "Real User"): A real JinnLogger contact with their own account.
*   **Local User** (gray badge, "Virtual User"): A placeholder created only within your project.

---

## 🗑️ Removing Members
To remove a member from a project:
1. Find them in the table.
2. Click the **trash icon** in the Actions column.
3. Confirm the removal.

> **⚠️ Note:** You cannot remove the project **OWNER**. The owner is always the person who created the project.

## 🌐 JinnLoggers: Your Professional Network

Go to the **"JinnLoggers"** page in the sidebar. This is where you manage your network of contacts.

### Left Column: Your Network

#### Incoming Requests
If someone has sent you a connection request, you'll see an **"Incoming Requests"** card with:
*   The person's name and username.
*   **Accept** button (green ✓): Add them to your network.
*   **Reject** button (red ✗): Decline the request.

#### Your JinnLoggers
A list of all your connected contacts, each showing:
*   Avatar (initials circle).
*   Display name and username.
*   **Remove** button (red person icon): Disconnect from this contact.

### Right Column: Find & Connect

#### Search JinnLoggers
*   Type a **username or email** in the search field.
*   Click the **Search** button (or press Enter).
*   Results appear below. Click **"Connect"** (person-plus icon) to send a request.

#### Sent Requests
Below the search results, you can see **pending outgoing requests** with a "Pending" badge.
